What changed, and why

1
One timeline, not chat + hidden tabs. Everything the agent does — messages, plan, tools, edits, sub-agents, approvals — is a single chronological stream. The work is the centerpiece, chat is woven in.
2
Sub-agents are first-class. Each spawn is a nested lane with its own tool calls, tokens, cost and duration — lineage color carries into the inspector. This is the end-to-end observability piece.
3
Edits preview on click. Any edit card or workspace file opens in the inspector's Preview segment — diffs, images, HTML, markdown.
4
Usage is provider-reported. Real prompt/completion tokens from LiteLLM usage objects, real cost, per-turn breakdown — no more "estimated" placeholders, plus enforced budget caps.
5
Approvals happen in place. Policy gates render in the timeline at the moment they fire, with the reason spelled out — no modal, no separate approvals queue.
6
Three inspector segments replace five clipped tabs. Overview (usage + sub-agents + latency), Workspace (file tree), Preview (context-sensitive). Settings moves to the rail.
7
Fleet is a page, not a footer row. One OTP flow pairs both devices (phone, laptop) and harnesses (pi, opencode, custom adapters), with scope chosen at pairing time and revocation per device.
8
Policies are legible. A capability × mode matrix plus enforced budgets and per-device overrides — the authorization story in one screen.
9
Reasoning is a per-turn accordion. Collapsed by default, with its own token count, and excluded from TTS. Sub-agents' reasoning appears inside their trace.
10
Trace = end-to-end sub-agent observability. "Full trace" on any sub-agent opens its task, its own reasoning + tool timeline, and its own metrics — persisted, not just a session rollup.
11
Settings is one page on the rail — models, prompt directives, skills (attach per session from the composer), voice, memory. Nothing hidden in a sixth clipped tab.
12
Connectors are their own page. MCP servers (github, lightpanda browser, slack…) registered once, shared by every harness, with per-tool allow/ask/block. Skills = instructions; connectors = tools.
13
Mission is a lens, not a tab. Deep-effort sessions get a zoomed-out view of the same timeline data: the agent's own phase breakdown, task ownership per sub-agent, cross-check gates, and re-plans tagged with the reasoning turn that caused them. No second source of truth.
15
Compact composer, everything an accordion chip. Mode, prompt, effort and skills are uniform popover chips — one quiet row. The prompt chip picks from a stored library of frontier-style system prompts (claude/gemini/codex flavors) that the agent and its sub-agents inherit.
14
Effort ≠ permission. fast / balanced / deep is a composer profile (model routing + reasoning budget); Chat/Plan/Edit/Yolo stays what the agent may do. Two axes, not seven modes.
Mobile IA mirrors the rail: Console / Fleet / Policies as bottom nav; inspector segments become swipe-up sheets. One machine can run any number of harnesses — each is a separate row in Fleet with its own policy scope.
